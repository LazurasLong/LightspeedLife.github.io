Wow, so I got so consumed by NaNo I didn't post to the website in a month. I suppose that's a good thing. I'm gonna try to take it as one. I don't think I wrote 50'000 words in the last 30 days, though it is time-consuming to count, as I write almost entirely longhand, and must transcribe my illegibly fine print before running *wc*.

I have nonetheless written at a quite ferocious pace, and accumulated a great deal of scribbled-up pages in my Black Books. In addition, loose papers fill my office, covered in dense scrawl or ink prints. There are volumes here. The thought that I might collect and finilize, beautify, and legibify them in some sort of structured codex, as a final, publishable book.


It is also important to finish a work, and then keep making more; not to stay with the past too long. So that is what I will do.


This last month has taught me about next steps: Arranging my life so that I don't feel lost. I know where I am and where I'm going because I know where my next step is, and I remember my last. As far as next steps go, one thing I really must do is redo my website. I am thinking of perhaps using jekyll to make things easier on me, but regardless--big layout changes, and maybe I can make this look good on everyone's device, and most importantly, I'll be adding more images. I need to break up the space--the expansive black field of hyperspace. There is a point where it's too much fuzz. As a matter of fact, that's one of the main reasons for this splash post.
