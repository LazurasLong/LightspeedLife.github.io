No time to worry about that.

The police were ten steps behind, guns raised and aiming. I slipped off toward the street below. Whoosh! The air picked up speed around me as I plummeted, tossing my hair and coat about, and the wind was so loud I didn't hear them shooting at me from behind me on the balcony. Forgot they were there altogether until a bullet whizzed past my peripherals, nearly taking my ear! I pulled the ripcord on my harness and activated the jetpack. Since I was facing down, it kicked me forward into a tumble into the empty space above the square, some 400 feet below. More gunshots above. I noticed them now, but they were cops! I looked back as I tumbled. Their outlines were dark against a dark sky, and it was hard to see clearly, buty they couldn't be cops. Cops never go out for blood unless you took one of their own, and though I couldn't make out the details, there were too many on that rooftop. Had to be a sting. S.W.A.T. fuck. I fired my jets again halfway through the turn, changing my trajectory, but I was still in the open. SWAT don't miss often. Didn't want to think about what would happen if they missed again and hit my jetpack... Not in that moment. It was a long way down. I kepped shooting my jetpack in bursts, jinking this way and that, making it hard for their computer brains to work out a firing solution for me, but I was quickly losing control myself! And I was tumbling so much I wouldn't be able to make it back in toward the building like I had planned. I'd already missed my window, but I got lucky again, and my exit came in the form of instats flying through the causeways at the last possible moment, and at a decent clip, too, which meant I had to get up to speed with them, which meant I had to go in a straight line and make SWAT's job a little easier. I jet left and up, trying to spiral as much as possible around all the bullets flying around me. Oh man, I could feel them at my back. I was just glad they weren't in my back. The first craft came 'round, going at waaay too fast a clip for me to catch. Bastards...

"Slow down for air traffic!" I quoted the velocity warning signs posted around the tunnels and byways of the metro. I'd have to catch the next one, and fast, and hope the SWAT didn't assume we were accomplices and just shoot us both down, me and the craft. The first craft whizzed past me, but caught me in his updraft, and I spun into the virtual lane.

Thanks I guess. Anything to help make me a harder target for those SWAT pigs. But now the other interstats were swerving, trying to pull away from me. I was still tumbling and they were getting farther away from me as they neared. I puffed my jets a few times and did sommersaults, but still, now the craft were really pulling away from me. But oh god did I get lucky. A stray shot clipped the stabilizer of the craft closest to me and sent it tumbling in my direction!

Ohthankgodohgodohgodthankyouthank--

I caught on to his passenger door as he careened by and hoped the pigs wouldn't blast us both. Oh fuck. The door nearly took my hand off, even as fast as I had been going relative to the intersat. Well, I caught it, and now we were past the corner of another building. It would take them a minute to mobilize an air squad, and I wondered if I should bail, and ditch the jetpack somewhere and just not go through with the mission at all. Where did all this heat come from? It wasn't like the authorities to send a kill squad after a guy like me. Something must have really riled them. I was thinking all this when I caught the driver's eye through the cockpit and I saw that he was looking at me.

"Well, are you gonna get in? Get in!"

I climbed in the cab, which was difficult as my only handhold was also the door I had to open.

"I had a feeling--I knew it was you the moment I saw you. Just had to be, I had a hunch."

I was still too tossed about to talk. My stomach felt like it was in my mouth. Wich... Well, I was hoping it wouldn't be.

"You're him, right? You're that kid who's been doing the art bomb scenes around Drisgall?"

My mouth started to form the word for "who?" But he continued.

"I'm a huge fan! Ya know, fuck the government and everything. Pigs!"

I had felt lucky he talked enough for the both of us, but my mouth was late on the response.

"Who?" I choked a little bit and hold my breath till my stomach settled.

"You know, the guy been doin' all them paintings across the city--he goes out nights and sets up bombs, see, so as he don't get caught, but they explode with paint, not fire and he loads some reall jazz in em! Ain't you that guy?"

Now here I had a chance to remain low-key, if there was still meaning for such a phrase after these last 10 minutes, but again my dumb old mouth was slow on the roll. Just had to spit somethin' out. But he cut me off anyway.

"Yeah that's--"

"I knew it had to be you. I mean who else would be flyin around the skyways like that, with the pigs on him an' all..."

True.

I finally started to come to my senses. "Who are you?"

"Oh! 'Scuse me dude, name's Abar. It's a pleasure to finally meet you, man!"

I was almost bummed I couldn't introduce him to the fanclub.

"Oh I work over in the PANHAM district. I was on my way back when I saw you tumbling by and thought you could use a lift, dude."

"Ha Ha!" It was the first honest laugh I'd had since the feds showed up. And I suddenly felt much more in my own skin. "That I did, too. I was tumbling. They nearly got me more than a couple times."

"Looks like they did, son."
